Date.april_twelfth

For my adversarial piece of clothing, I decided to draw a hoodie with a bunch of faces on it. At the beginning of the article, it talks a lot about facial recognition, so I thought putting a bunch of extreamly realistic faces on a hoodie may be able to trick facial recognition cameras/software into thinking the faces on the hoodie are real people, and making it more likely that cameras will pick up these fake faces than a person's real face.
